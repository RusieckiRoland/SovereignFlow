# Regulation on Critical Infrastructure Data Governance (RCIDG)
## Article 1 — General Provisions

This Regulation establishes the framework for data governance within critical infrastructure
organisations. It defines obligations for the protection, classification, and controlled disclosure
of operational data generated by or relating to critical infrastructure systems.

### 1.1 Purpose

The purpose of this Regulation is to ensure that operational data is handled in a manner that:

- prevents unauthorised access or disclosure to parties without a legitimate need to know;
- preserves the integrity and availability of information required for continuity of operations;
- enables auditable, traceable decision-making in response to incidents and regulatory enquiries.

### 1.2 Scope

This Regulation applies to all entities designated as critical infrastructure operators under
Article 2, and to third parties that process operational data on their behalf. It covers data
generated during normal operations, maintenance activities, incident response, and regulatory
reporting.

### 1.3 Relationship to Other Instruments

This Regulation supplements and does not replace sector-specific security requirements. Where a
conflict exists between this Regulation and a binding sector instrument, the more restrictive
requirement applies. Operators must document any such conflicts and the rationale for their chosen
approach in their Data Governance Register (see Article 6).
