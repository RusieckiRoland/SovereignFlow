# Article 15 — Commencement and Transitional Provisions

### 15.1 Entry into Force

This Regulation enters into force on the date of its publication in the Official Gazette.

### 15.2 Transitional Period

Critical Infrastructure Operators that are subject to this Regulation at the date of entry into
force are granted a transitional period to achieve full compliance:

- **12 months**: Articles 3.1 (classification obligation), 3.2 (register obligation), and
  Article 6 (Data Governance Register structure) must be complied with.

- **18 months**: Articles 7 (access control), 5 (risk assessment), and 9 (third-party processors)
  must be complied with.

- **24 months**: Articles 8 (classified handling), 14 (technical standards), and 11 (retention)
  must be complied with.

Operators that are newly designated as Critical Infrastructure Operators after the date of entry
into force must comply with all provisions of this Regulation within 12 months of designation.

### 15.3 Existing Controls

Where an operator can demonstrate that an existing control satisfies the substantive requirement
of this Regulation, the Competent Authority may accept the existing control as compliant even if
it does not precisely follow the form specified herein. Operators seeking such recognition must
apply in writing within 6 months of entry into force.

### 15.4 Review

The Competent Authority must conduct a review of the effectiveness of this Regulation no later
than 3 years after the date of entry into force, and publish a report of its findings and any
proposed amendments.
