# Article 13 — Appeals Procedure

### 13.1 Right of Appeal

Any Critical Infrastructure Operator subject to a decision by the Competent Authority under
Article 12 has the right to appeal that decision. The appeal must be lodged within 30 days
of receipt of the written decision.

### 13.2 Grounds for Appeal

Permissible grounds for appeal include:

- error of fact: the decision was based on facts that are materially incorrect;
- error of law: the decision misapplied a provision of this Regulation or a higher instrument;
- procedural irregularity: the investigation that led to the decision failed to follow a
  mandatory procedural requirement, causing material prejudice to the operator;
- proportionality: the penalty imposed is disproportionate to the breach having regard to the
  mitigating factors specified in Article 12.3.

### 13.3 Tribunal Procedure

The designated administrative tribunal must:

1. acknowledge receipt of the appeal within 5 working days;
2. notify the Competent Authority and invite a response within 20 working days;
3. hold a hearing within 60 days of receipt unless both parties agree to a decision on the papers;
4. issue a reasoned decision within 30 days of the hearing.

### 13.4 Outcomes

The tribunal may:

- uphold the Competent Authority's decision in full;
- vary the decision, including reducing or increasing a fine within the applicable range;
- quash the decision and remit it to the Competent Authority for reconsideration;
- substitute its own decision.

The tribunal's decision is final on questions of fact. Questions of law may be further appealed
to the courts of competent jurisdiction within 30 days of the tribunal's decision.

### 13.5 Confidentiality

Proceedings before the tribunal involving CLASSIFIED information are conducted in closed session.
The tribunal's published decision must redact any information that would breach the classification
requirements of Article 4 or the handling requirements of Article 8.
