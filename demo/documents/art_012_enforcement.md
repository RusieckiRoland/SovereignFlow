# Article 12 — Enforcement and Penalties

### 12.1 Competent Authority Powers

The Competent Authority may, following an investigation:

- issue a formal warning for first-time or minor non-compliance;
- issue a compliance notice specifying corrective actions and a deadline;
- impose an administrative fine;
- require the appointment of an independent compliance monitor at the operator's expense;
- recommend to the relevant licensing authority the suspension or revocation of the operator's
  designation as a Critical Infrastructure Operator.

### 12.2 Administrative Fines

Administrative fines under this Regulation are graduated by the severity of the breach:

**Level 1** (minor, technical): up to 0.5% of annual turnover or EUR 500,000, whichever is
greater. Applies to: failure to maintain the Register in the required format; late submission
of the Register on request; failure to complete training records.

**Level 2** (significant): up to 2% of annual turnover or EUR 2,000,000, whichever is greater.
Applies to: failure to conduct mandatory Risk Assessments; failure to implement required
technical controls for RESTRICTED data; failure to notify a Tier 2 incident within the required
timeline.

**Level 3** (serious): up to 4% of annual turnover or EUR 10,000,000, whichever is greater.
Applies to: unauthorised disclosure of CLASSIFIED data attributable to a failure of required
controls; failure to notify a Tier 3 incident; obstruction of a Competent Authority investigation.

### 12.3 Mitigating Factors

In determining the level of a fine, the Competent Authority shall take into account:

- the promptness with which the operator detected and contained the breach;
- the degree to which the operator cooperated with the investigation;
- evidence of proactive measures taken beyond the requirements of this Regulation;
- the history of compliance of the operator.

### 12.4 Appeals

An operator may appeal any decision of the Competent Authority to the designated administrative
tribunal within 30 days of receipt of the decision. The appeal suspends enforcement of the
decision, except where the Competent Authority certifies that immediate action is required to
prevent serious harm. Appeals are governed by Article 13.
