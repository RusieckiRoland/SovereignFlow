# Article 11 — Retention and Disposal

### 11.1 Retention Periods

Unless a longer period is required by sector-specific regulation or ongoing legal proceedings,
operational data must be retained for the following minimum periods from the date of creation:

| Classification | Minimum Retention |
|---------------|-------------------|
| PUBLIC        | 2 years           |
| INTERNAL      | 5 years           |
| RESTRICTED    | 7 years           |
| CLASSIFIED    | 10 years, or as directed by Competent Authority |

Data subject to an active investigation or legal hold must not be disposed of until the hold
is lifted by written authorisation from the Data Custodian and, where applicable, legal counsel.

### 11.2 Retention Review

Before the expiry of the retention period, the Data Custodian must conduct a retention review
to determine whether:

- a sector-specific or legal requirement mandates continued retention;
- an ongoing investigation or legal proceeding creates a hold;
- the data retains operational value that justifies voluntary extended retention.

Where none of these conditions applies, the data must be scheduled for disposal.

### 11.3 Disposal Procedures

Disposal must be carried out in accordance with Article 8.5 for CLASSIFIED data. For RESTRICTED
data, electronic disposal must use a secure overwrite method meeting the relevant national standard.
For INTERNAL and PUBLIC data, standard media erasure is acceptable, provided the media is not
re-used in a context where the erased data could cause harm if recovered.

Disposal events must be recorded in the Data Governance Register (Article 6) including the
method used, the volume disposed of, and the authorising Data Custodian.
