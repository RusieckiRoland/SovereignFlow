# Article 5 — Risk Assessment

### 5.1 Mandatory Assessment Cycle

Critical Infrastructure Operators must conduct a Data Governance Risk Assessment at intervals not
exceeding 12 months, or following any material change to their data landscape, including:

- acquisition of a new system that generates operational data;
- a change in classification level for a dataset covering more than 1,000 records;
- a cyber incident that resulted in confirmed or suspected unauthorised access to RESTRICTED
  or CLASSIFIED data.

### 5.2 Assessment Scope

The Risk Assessment must evaluate:

1. **Exposure risk**: The probability and impact of unauthorised disclosure for each dataset
   classified as RESTRICTED or CLASSIFIED (see Article 4.3 and 4.4).

2. **Integrity risk**: The probability and impact of unauthorised modification of data that
   underpins operational decision-making.

3. **Availability risk**: The probability and impact of loss of access to data that is required
   for continuity of operations or regulatory reporting.

4. **Supply chain risk**: The extent to which third-party processors contribute to the above
   risks, and the contractual and technical controls in place to mitigate them.

### 5.3 Assessment Output

The output of the Risk Assessment is a Risk Register, which must be included in the Data Governance
Register (see Article 3.2). The Risk Register must identify, for each risk:

- a severity rating (LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH / CRITICAL);
- the mitigating controls in place;
- any residual risk and the basis for accepting it;
- the owner responsible for tracking the risk to closure.

### 5.4 Assessment by Third Parties

Operators may commission their Risk Assessments from accredited third-party assessors. The
operator remains fully responsible for the accuracy of the assessment and for implementing
identified mitigations.
