# Article 9 — Third-Party Data Processors

### 9.1 Contractual Requirements

Before transferring operational data to a third-party processor, a Critical Infrastructure Operator
must have in place a binding Data Processing Agreement that:

- specifies the categories and classification levels of data to be processed;
- prohibits the processor from using the data for any purpose other than the contracted service;
- requires the processor to apply controls at least as stringent as those required by this
  Regulation for the relevant classification level;
- grants the operator audit rights exercisable on reasonable notice;
- requires the processor to notify the operator within 24 hours of any incident affecting the
  operator's data;
- provides for secure deletion or return of the data on termination of the contract.

### 9.2 Prohibited Transfers

Third-party processors may not sub-contract processing of RESTRICTED or CLASSIFIED data without
prior written consent from the operator and notification to the Competent Authority. Any such
sub-processor is subject to the same requirements as the primary processor under Article 9.1.

### 9.3 Processor Audit Rights

Operators must audit the data governance practices of processors handling RESTRICTED or CLASSIFIED
data at intervals not exceeding 24 months. Audit reports must be retained and included in the
Data Governance Register. Where an audit identifies material non-compliance, the operator must
notify the Competent Authority within 30 days.

### 9.4 Cloud and Managed Services

The use of cloud services for storage or processing of RESTRICTED or CLASSIFIED data requires
prior approval from the Competent Authority. Applications must include a technical description
of the data flows, the encryption mechanisms in use, and evidence that the service meets the
requirements of Article 7.5 (technical enforcement) and Article 8 (classified handling).
