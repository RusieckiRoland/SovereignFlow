# Article 2 — Definitions

For the purposes of this Regulation, the following definitions apply:

**Critical Infrastructure Operator (CIO)**: An entity that owns, operates, or is responsible for
a system or service whose disruption would have significant adverse effects on public safety,
economic stability, or national security. Designation is made by the competent national authority.

**Operational Data**: Any data generated, collected, or processed in the course of operating
critical infrastructure. This includes telemetry, event logs, maintenance records, incident
reports, configuration states, and performance metrics.

**Data Classification Level**: A label assigned to operational data that indicates the sensitivity
of the data and the minimum controls required for its handling. The four levels defined in this
Regulation are: PUBLIC, INTERNAL, RESTRICTED, and CLASSIFIED.

**Data Custodian**: The organisational role responsible for the day-to-day governance of a defined
dataset, including classification, access grant decisions, and retention management.

**Access Control List (ACL)**: A mechanism that specifies which organisational roles or external
entities are permitted to access a given dataset or document.

**Incident**: Any event that disrupts, or has the potential to disrupt, the availability, integrity,
or confidentiality of critical infrastructure systems or their operational data.

**Competent Authority**: The governmental or regulatory body designated to oversee compliance with
this Regulation within a given jurisdiction or sector.
