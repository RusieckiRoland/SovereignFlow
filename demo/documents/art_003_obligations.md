# Article 3 — Operator Obligations

Critical Infrastructure Operators have the following standing obligations under this Regulation:

### 3.1 Classification Obligation

All operational data must be assigned a classification level (see Article 4) within 30 days of
its creation or receipt. Data that has not been classified must be treated as RESTRICTED until
a Data Custodian assigns a definitive level.

### 3.2 Register Obligation

Each operator must maintain a Data Governance Register containing:

- an inventory of all datasets under their custody;
- the classification level and rationale for each dataset;
- the access control lists in force for each dataset;
- a record of changes to classification or access controls, including the authorising Data Custodian
  and the date of the change.

The Register must be made available to the Competent Authority on request within 5 working days.

### 3.3 Training Obligation

All personnel with access to INTERNAL, RESTRICTED, or CLASSIFIED data must complete an accredited
data governance training programme. Training must be refreshed at intervals not exceeding 24 months.
Records of completion must be retained for at least 5 years and included in the Data Governance
Register.

### 3.4 Incident Notification Obligation

Operators must notify the Competent Authority of any incident affecting RESTRICTED or CLASSIFIED
data within 72 hours of detection. The notification must include: a description of the incident,
the classification level of the affected data, the estimated number of affected records, and
immediate remediation actions taken. Full incident reports are governed by Article 10.
