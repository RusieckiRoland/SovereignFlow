# Article 6 — Data Governance Register

### 6.1 Register Structure

The Data Governance Register is a structured record that operators must maintain in a format
approved by the Competent Authority. At minimum it must contain the following sections:

**Section A — Dataset Inventory**
For each dataset: unique identifier, description, owning business unit, Data Custodian,
classification level, volume (approximate record count or data volume), retention period,
and storage location.

**Section B — Access Control Records**
For each dataset: the current ACL, the date the ACL was last reviewed, the name of the
authorising Data Custodian, and a log of all access grant and revocation events in the
preceding 24 months.

**Section C — Risk Register**
As defined in Article 5.3.

**Section D — Incident Log**
A chronological record of all incidents affecting data classified RESTRICTED or above,
with the notification status for each (see Article 3.4) and the outcome of the
full incident report (see Article 10).

**Section E — Training Records**
Evidence of completion of mandatory training for all personnel with access to INTERNAL
or above data, as required by Article 3.3.

### 6.2 Register Integrity

The Register must be maintained in a tamper-evident format. Any modification to the Register
must be attributed to a named individual and timestamped. Deletion of records from the Register
is not permitted; superseded records must be marked as superseded and retained.

### 6.3 Register Availability

Operators must ensure the Register remains available for regulatory inspection at all times.
A copy of the Register must be stored at a geographically separate location from the primary
copy, and tested for recoverability at intervals not exceeding 6 months.
